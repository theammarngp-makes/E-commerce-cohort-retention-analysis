# Cohort Retention Insights

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Sharp Drop After First Month
Customer retention drops drastically after Month 1 across almost all cohorts.  
This indicates that most users do not return after their first purchase.

### 2. Extremely Low Retention Rates
Retention percentages are very low (generally below 1%), showing that only a very small fraction of customers make repeat purchases.

### 3. One-Time Buyer Dominance
The data suggests that the majority of customers are one-time buyers, highlighting weak customer loyalty.

### 4. Inconsistent Return Behavior
Some customers return after long gaps (e.g., Month 6, Month 10, etc.), but these cases are rare and not consistent.

### 5. Similar Patterns Across Cohorts
All cohorts (from 2016 to 2018) show similar retention behavior, indicating that the issue is systemic rather than time-specific or seasonal.

## 📈 Business Interpretation

- The business is heavily dependent on acquiring new customers  
- Customer retention strategies are currently ineffective  
- Repeat purchase behavior is very limited  
- Long-term customer value is low  

##  Recommendations

- Introduce loyalty programs to encourage repeat purchases  
- Implement targeted email/SMS marketing campaigns  
- Provide discounts or offers for returning customers  
- Improve overall customer experience (delivery, product quality, support)  
- Focus on retention as a key KPI instead of only acquisition
